# 01 · HR Round

> The first or last call. Low-stakes if you've prepared 6 answers; sudden if you haven't.

The HR round is almost never the round you *fail* — it's the round that quietly determines your level, comp band, and whether the recruiter advocates for you internally. Treat it as a structured conversation with predictable questions, not as a screening filter. Six rehearsed answers cover roughly 90% of what gets asked across companies.

## How HR actually evaluates you

Recruiters listen for three things and nothing else: (1) is your trajectory and target band consistent with what the team is hiring for, (2) are there any red flags around tenure, motivation, or interpersonal patterns, and (3) will you accept an offer if extended. Almost every question you'll hear maps to one of these. Answering "tell me about yourself" with a 4-minute career monologue signals lack of judgment; answering it with a tight 75-second arc signals senior-level communication.

## The cheat sheet

| Question | Time budget | Real test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tell me about yourself | 60-90s | Can you self-edit? |
| Strengths | 30-45s | Specific & relevant |
| Weakness | 45-60s | Self-aware + acting on it |
| Why this company | 45-60s | Did you do homework |
| Why leaving | 30-45s | No bitterness, clear pull factor |
| Conflict story | 90-120s | Empathy + ownership |
| Questions for us | 5-10min | What do you actually care about |
