# Interview Experiences by Company

> Anonymized notes on what each interview loop actually looks like — round count, focus areas, vibe, and the few questions that recur.

!!! warning "Loop structures change"
    These notes synthesize publicly available info (Glassdoor, LeetCode discussions, blog posts, friends' experiences from the last 12-18 months). Always cross-check with your recruiter the week before. Treat this as a starting prior, not gospel.

## What this section is

A pattern map. For each company below, you get:

- The shape of the loop (how many rounds, how long, what each one tests).
- What's culturally specific (e.g., Amazon LPs, Netflix culture deck, Meesho scale problems).
- The themes that keep showing up across reported experiences.
- A short "how to prep" list tuned for that bar.

It is not:

- A leak. We don't list "actual" questions — only generic forms that have appeared often enough to be safely public.
- A guarantee. Loops vary by team, level, and recruiter cohort. A staff loop at Google looks very different from an L4 loop.
- A substitute for asking your recruiter directly what your specific schedule looks like.

## How to read each page

Each company page is ~700 words and follows the same template — loop breakdown, what's different here, common themes, sample questions, prep notes, recent vibe. You can skim three pages in 15 minutes and walk away with a usable mental model.

If you're prepping for a specific company, read its page first, then go back and read 1-2 adjacent ones (e.g., prepping for Meesho? also read Flipkart and Swiggy — Indian product-engineering loops rhyme).

## Cross-cutting patterns

A few things show up almost everywhere — worth internalizing once and reusing:

- **One DSA round is almost always non-negotiable.** Even staff loops usually have at least one coding round. Don't skip DSA prep thinking your HLD is strong enough.
- **HLD bar scales with level.** Senior+ loops will push into capacity numbers, failure modes, and tradeoff justification. Junior loops mostly want a clean component diagram.
- **Behavioral matters more than candidates think.** Most rejections at senior+ levels are behavioral or "bar raiser" calls, not technical ones. Have your STAR stories ready.
- **Most loops have one "wildcard" round.** Could be Googleyness, Bar Raiser, hiring-manager fit, or skip-level. Hard to prep for technically — be yourself, ask good questions, and don't be a jerk.
- **The recruiter call is signal.** If they tell you a round is "casual" or "low-stakes," it's not. It still has a thumbs-up/thumbs-down outcome.

## What to do after reading

1. Pick 2-3 target companies, read their pages.
2. Map their loop onto your prep calendar — which weeks emphasize DSA, which emphasize HLD, which emphasize behavioral.
3. Run mocks specifically in the style of your top target. A Google-style mock and a Stripe-style mock are very different exercises.
4. The week of your loop, **ask your recruiter to confirm the exact round list**. Notes here can drift; recruiter info is the ground truth.

Good luck. The bar is real, but it's beatable with deliberate prep.
