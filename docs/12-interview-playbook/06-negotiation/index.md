# 06 · Negotiation

> The 30-minute conversation that pays for the next two years. Know your numbers, know their levers, never accept on the call.

Most engineers under-negotiate by tens of thousands of dollars (or lakhs of rupees) over a 4-year tenure simply because they take the first number, or they don't realize what's actually moveable. This sub-section gives you the mental model.

## Three principles

1. **Never accept on the call.** "Thank you, this is exciting. Give me a few days to review with my family / partner / advisor." Always.
2. **Negotiate the total package, not the base.** Base, sign-on, equity, target bonus, equity refresh, level, start date, RSU vesting cadence — every line item is a lever.
3. **Be polite and specific.** "Based on competing offers and the market for this level, can we get base to $X and sign-on to $Y?" beats "Can you do better?"

## When NOT to negotiate hard

- Tiny startup, you're employee #1-5, founder has limited room and you want a strong relationship from day zero. (Still negotiate — but be light.)
- You're transitioning from an unrelated field and they're taking a real bet on you.
- The package is already obviously above market and the recruiter has been straight with you the whole way.

Even in these cases, you should still **understand** the package fully and ask for one or two small adjustments — a sign-on, an early review, a title bump. Recruiters expect *some* push; accepting silently can actually weaken their internal advocacy for you.
