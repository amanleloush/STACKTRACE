# Coding Interview Framework

> How to drive 45 minutes of live coding without panicking — clarify, plan, code, test, optimize.

A coding interview is not a LeetCode contest. It's a structured conversation in which you demonstrate that you can take an ambiguous problem, narrow it down, build a working solution, and reason about its costs — all while typing into a shared editor with someone watching. The candidates who fail rarely fail because they can't code. They fail because they jump to coding before thinking, then thrash for 30 minutes without a plan.

The pages below give you a repeatable framework. Internalize it once and every coding round looks the same: same shape, same beats, same recovery moves when something goes wrong.

## How to use this folder

Read all four pages in order before your next loop. Then, in mock interviews, force yourself through the framework verbatim — including saying "let me clarify a few things first" out loud — until it feels natural. The point of a framework is that under stress your brain stops generating new ideas and reaches for cached patterns. Make this the cached pattern.

## What good looks like

A senior engineer in a coding interview spends the first 8 minutes not writing code. They restate the problem, ask 3-5 clarifying questions, walk through 2 examples on the whiteboard, propose 2 approaches with their complexities, get buy-in on one, and then code. By the 40-minute mark they're testing on the original example and a tricky edge case. By 45 they're talking about how they'd scale it or what they'd improve. That's the loop you're rehearsing.
