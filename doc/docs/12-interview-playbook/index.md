## How to use this section

The interview loop is a sequence of independent filters, and each one rewards different prep. Treat this section like a checklist you walk through in order rather than a book to read cover-to-cover.

1. **Start with 01 and 02 to build your story bank.** The HR round and the behavioral round draw from the same 6-8 STAR stories. If you pre-write these once — leadership, failure, conflict, impact, ambiguity, learning — you stop improvising under pressure and the same material covers every "tell me about a time when..." prompt in every company's loop.
2. **Read 03 and 04 before any technical screen or onsite.** These pages give you the *frame* — what to say in the first 60 seconds, when to write code vs talk, how to recover when stuck. The frame is more important than knowing every algorithm, because a strong frame turns a 60% solution into a hire signal.
3. **Skim 05 the week before each onsite.** The company-specific pages document the format (number of rounds, interviewer style, what kinds of follow-ups get asked) so you can calibrate your prep. There's no point grinding hard Leetcode for a company that asks practical engineering questions, or vice versa.
4. **Open 06 only when an offer arrives.** Reading the negotiation page during your prep cycle creates anxiety; reading it after a verbal offer turns a moment of panic into a 30-minute scripted conversation that's usually worth 10-20% of base.

A common failure mode is treating these rounds as obstacles to "get through" instead of signals you control. Every page in this section is written assuming you'll spend roughly half your prep time on the technical rounds and half on everything else — because the data says that's roughly where the offer/no-offer line lives at the senior level.
